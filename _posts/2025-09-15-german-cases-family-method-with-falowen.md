## The Family Idea

In German, **every statement or question belongs to a case family**:
**Nominative**, **Accusative**, **Dative**, or **Genitive**.  
Verbs and prepositions also “live” in families. Once you know the family, you pick the **right articles** — if not, the sentence breaks.

Falowen teaches you to **find the family first**, then choose the article and check with guided discussion in **Custom Chat** so the rule becomes a habit.

---

## Four Families — clear and practical

> Think in **families**. Find the family → choose the article → you’re correct.

### Nominative (subject family)
- **Meaning:** the subject of the sentence. With **sein / werden / bleiben**, the noun after the verb is **also nominative** (predicate nominative) — there is **no object** in these sentences.
- **Questions:** *Wer?* / *Was?*
- **Examples:**
  - **Der Mann** kommt.
  - **Das ist** **mein Bruder**. (sein → both sides nominative)

### Accusative (direct-object family)
- **Meaning:** the direct object; also fixed by certain prepositions.
- **Verb signals:** **sehen, haben, finden, kaufen, besuchen, mögen** → take an accusative object.
- **Prepositions (accusative-only):** **durch, für, gegen, ohne, um**
- **Example:** Ich **sehe** **einen Hund**. (sehen → Akkusativ)

### Dative (receiver family)
- **Meaning:** the receiver/indirect object; also fixed by certain prepositions.
- **Verb signals:** **helfen, danken, gehören, gefallen, gratulieren, antworten** → take Dativ.
- **Prepositions (dative-only):** **aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu, gegenüber**
- **Example:** Ich **helfe** **dem Mann**. (helfen → Dativ)

### Genitive (possession / “whose”)
- **Meaning:** possession/relationship; also fixed by certain prepositions.
- **Prepositions (genitive):** **trotz, während, wegen, statt**
- **Example:** Das Auto **des Nachbarn**.

---

## Prepositions: three types (decide the case fast)

1. **Accusative-only** → next noun is **Akkusativ**  
   **durch, für, gegen, ohne, um**

2. **Dative-only** → next noun is **Dativ**  
   **aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu, gegenüber**

3. **Two-case (Wechselpräpositionen)** → choose **Akkusativ** or **Dativ** by **movement vs. location**  
   **an, auf, in, neben, über, unter, vor, zwischen**
   - **Movement / change of place** → **Akkusativ**  
     *Ich gehe **in die** Schule.* (movement → Akk. Fem.)
   - **Location / no movement** → **Dativ**  
     *Ich bin **in der** Schule.* (location → Dat. Fem.)

More two-case examples:
- *Ich stelle die Flasche **auf den** Tisch.* (movement → Akk.)  
  *Die Flasche steht **auf dem** Tisch.* (location → Dat.)
- *Ich hänge das Bild **an die** Wand.* (movement → Akk.)  
  *Das Bild hängt **an der** Wand.* (location → Dat.)

---

## Decide in this order (A1–B1 friendly)

1) **Is there a preposition? Identify its type first.**  
   - Accusative-only → the next noun is **Akkusativ**.  
   - Dative-only → the next noun is **Dativ**.  
   - Two-case → check meaning: **movement → Akkusativ**, **location → Dativ**.

2) **No preposition? Check the verb.**  
   - In your **Akkusativ list** (e.g., *sehen, haben, finden…*) → noun is **Akkusativ**.  
   - In your **Dativ list** (e.g., *helfen, danken, gehören…*) → noun is **Dativ**.  
   - **sein / werden / bleiben** → **Nominativ on both sides** (no object).

3) **Pick the article + endings.**  
   Use the tables; remember Genitiv **-s/-es** (masc./neut.) and Dativ plural **-n** on the noun when possible.

## Quick Tests (with answers)

> Use **definite articles** in this section.

### A) Fill in the article
1. Ich sehe ___ Mann.  
2. Das ist ___ Frau.  
3. Ich helfe ___ Kind.  
4. Das Auto ___ Nachbarn ist neu.  
5. Wir gehen in ___ Park.  
6. Wir sind in ___ Park.  
7. Er gibt ___ Frau ___ Blumen.  
8. Ohne ___ Pass komme ich nicht rein.  
9. Mit ___ Freund gehe ich ins Kino.  
10. Ich habe ___ Buch.  
11. Sie wohnt bei ___ Tante.  

**Answers**  
1. den Mann (Akk. m)  
2. die Frau (Nom. f)  
3. dem Kind (Dat. n)  
4. der Nachbarn (Gen. Pl.)  
5. den Park (Akk. m, movement)  
6. dem Park (Dat. m, location)  
7. der Frau, die Blumen (Dat. f; Akk. Pl.)  
8. den Pass (Akk. m, ohne)  
9. dem Freund (Dat. m, mit)  
10. das Buch (Akk. n)  
11. der Tante (Dat. f, bei)

### B) Choose the family (write A, D, or G)
a) Wir warten **auf** den Bus.  
b) Ich komme **aus** der Stadt.  
c) **Trotz** des Regens spielen wir.  
d) Er stellt die Flasche **auf** den Tisch.  
e) Die Lampe steht **auf** dem Tisch.

**Answers**  
a) Accusative (fixed with <em>warten auf</em>)  
b) Dative (<em>aus</em>)  
c) Genitive (<em>trotz</em>)  
d) Accusative (movement onto)  
e) Dative (location on)

### C) Mini writing task
Write 4–6 lines about your room using at least three two-way prepositions (in, auf, neben, unter, vor, hinter).  
Then paste your draft into **Custom Chat** to discuss which families you used, why those articles fit, and how to improve the sentences.

### D) Two-case drill (movement vs. location)
1. Ich lege das Buch ___ Tisch.  
2. Das Buch liegt ___ Tisch.  
3. Er geht ___ Kino.  
4. Er ist ___ Kino.  
5. Wir hängen das Poster ___ Wand.  
6. Das Poster hängt ___ Wand.

**Answers**  
1. **auf den** Tisch (Akk., movement)  
2. **auf dem** Tisch (Dat., location)  
3. **ins** Kino = in **das** Kino (Akk., movement)  
4. **im** Kino = in **dem** Kino (Dat., location)  
5. **an die** Wand (Akk., movement)  
6. **an der** Wand (Dat., location)
