## German Alphabet for Complete Beginners

Learning the **German alphabet** is the first step to mastering pronunciation and spelling in German. This guide introduces you to the letters and their sounds, helping you build a strong foundation.

---

### The German Alphabet: Letters and Sounds

### Special Characters

German also uses three special characters not found in English:

- **Ä / ä** — sounds like *eh* in *bed*  
- **Ö / ö** — sounds like *i* in *bird* (without the ‘r’)  
- **Ü / ü** — sounds like *u* in *flute* but with rounded lips  

Plus, the **ß (Eszett or sharp S)** which sounds like a double “s”:

- Straße (street) — pronounced *shtrah-suh*

---

### Pronunciation Tips for Beginners

- **S at the beginning of words before vowels** sounds like “z”. Example: *Sonne* (sun) is pronounced “Zonne”.
- **W is pronounced like English “V”**. Example: *Wasser* (water) sounds like “Vasser”.
- **V is often pronounced like “F”**. Example: *Vogel* (bird) sounds like “Fogel”.
- Roll or gargle your **R** slightly, especially at the start of words.
- Practice saying each letter out loud slowly and clearly.
