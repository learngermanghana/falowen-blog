## German Sentence Structure for Beginners

Understanding **German sentence structure** is essential for building clear and correct sentences. In this lesson, we'll focus on the **basic word order** in simple sentences, which is a great starting point for beginners.

---

## Basic Word Order in German Sentences

The most common word order in simple German sentences is:

**Subject – Verb – (Time) – (Manner) – (Place) – Object**

### Key points:
- The **verb** is always the **second element** in a main clause.
- The **subject** usually comes first.
- Time, manner, and place adverbs follow in that order.
- The **object** often comes at the end unless it’s the first element for emphasis.

---

## Examples of Basic Word Order

1. **Ich lerne Deutsch.**  
   (I learn German.)  
   *Subject – Verb – Object*

2. **Heute spiele ich Fußball.**  
   (Today I play soccer.)  
   *Time – Verb – Subject – Object*

3. **Er liest jeden Tag ein Buch.**  
   (He reads a book every day.)  
   *Subject – Verb – Time – Object*

4. **Wir gehen morgen ins Kino.**  
   (We go to the cinema tomorrow.)  
   *Subject – Verb – Time – Place*

5. **Sie fährt mit dem Bus zur Arbeit.**  
   (She drives to work by bus.)  
   *Subject – Verb – Manner – Place*

6. **Am Wochenende besuche ich meine Freunde.**  
   (On the weekend, I visit my friends.)  
   *Time – Verb – Subject – Object*

---

## Tips for Beginners

- Always find the **verb** and make sure it stays in the second position.
- When starting a sentence with something other than the subject (like a time or place), the subject follows immediately after the verb.
- Practice by rearranging simple sentences and checking if the verb stays second.
- Use time expressions like **heute** (today), **morgen** (tomorrow), or **am Wochenende** (on the weekend) to practice sentence openings.
