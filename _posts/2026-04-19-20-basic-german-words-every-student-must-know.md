## 20 Basic German Words Every Student Must Know

Learning **basic German words** is the first step to confidently understanding and speaking the language. These foundational vocabulary items are essential for beginners and will help you navigate everyday conversations, classroom interactions, and simple reading materials.

### Why Focus on Basic German Words?

Starting with common, practical words allows you to:
- Build a strong vocabulary base
- Recognize patterns in sentence structure
- Gain confidence in speaking and listening

### 20 Essential Basic German Words

Here are 20 words every German learner should memorize early on:

#### Common Nouns
- **das Buch** — the book  
- **der Tisch** — the table  
- **die Schule** — the school  

#### Useful Verbs
- **sein** — to be  
- **haben** — to have  
- **gehen** — to go  

#### Everyday Adjectives and Adverbs
- **gut** — good  
- **schlecht** — bad  
- **jetzt** — now  

#### Basic Question Words
- **wer?** — who?  
- **was?** — what?  
- **wo?** — where?  

#### Important Pronouns
- **ich** — I  
- **du** — you (informal)  
- **er/sie/es** — he/she/it  

### Practical Tips for Memorizing Basic German Words

- **Use flashcards:** Write the German word on one side and the English meaning on the other.  
- **Practice daily:** Even 5–10 minutes a day helps retention.  
- **Make sentences:** Try to use new words in simple sentences to understand how they fit in context.  
- **Listen and repeat:** Hearing native speakers and repeating aloud improves pronunciation and memory.

### Next Steps

Once you feel comfortable with these words, you can start combining them to form basic sentences and questions. This will open doors to more complex conversations and better comprehension.
