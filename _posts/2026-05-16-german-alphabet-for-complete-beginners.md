## Day 32/180: German Alphabet for Complete Beginners

Learning the German alphabet is the first step to mastering reading, writing, and pronunciation. This guide introduces the letters, their sounds, and tips to help you get comfortable with the basics.

---

## The German Alphabet Overview

The German alphabet has **26 letters**, just like English, plus **4 special characters**:

- **26 standard letters:** A to Z
- **Special characters:** Ä, Ö, Ü, ß

| Letter | Pronunciation Example | Notes                     |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| A      | *ah* (as in *Mann*)  | Short /a/ sound           |
| E      | *eh* (as in *Bett*)  | Can be short or long      |
| I      | *ee* (as in *Kind*)  | Often a long /iː/ sound   |
| O      | *oh* (as in *Sohn*)  | Long or short /o/         |
| U      | *oo* (as in *Hund*)  | Long or short /u/         |
| ß      | *ss* (as in *Straße*)| Represents a double s     |

---

## Special German Letters and Their Sounds

### Umlauts (Ä, Ö, Ü)

These modify the vowel sound:

- **Ä / ä** — sounds like *e* in *bed*  
  Example: *Mädchen* (girl)
- **Ö / ö** — sounds like *i* in *sir* (rounded lips)  
  Example: *schön* (beautiful)
- **Ü / ü** — sounds like *ee* with rounded lips  
  Example: *Müll* (trash)

### The Eszett (ß)

- Represents a sharp *s* sound, like *ss* in *kiss*  
- Only used after long vowels or diphthongs  
- Example: *Straße* (street)

---

## Pronunciation Tips for Beginners

- **Vowels** can be short or long; length affects meaning.  
  Example: *bieten* (to offer) vs. *bitten* (to ask)
- **Consonants** generally sound like English but watch out for:
  - **W** pronounced like English *V*  
    Example: *Wasser* (water)
  - **V** pronounced like English *F*  
    Example: *Vater* (father)
  - **J** pronounced like English *Y*  
    Example: *Ja* (yes)
- Practice by saying letters aloud and comparing to examples.
