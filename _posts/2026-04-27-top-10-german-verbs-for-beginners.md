## Top 10 German Verbs for Beginners

Learning German verbs is a key step to building your language skills. Verbs are action words that help you express what you and others do every day. Here are the **top 10 German verbs for beginners** that you’ll use frequently.

### Why Start with These Verbs?

These verbs are essential because they:

- Appear often in daily conversations  
- Form the basis for many common sentences  
- Help you describe yourself, others, and your surroundings

### 1. Sein (to be)

One of the most important verbs in German. You use it to describe identity, state, or existence.

- Ich **bin** müde. (I am tired.)  
- Er **ist** Lehrer. (He is a teacher.)

### 2. Haben (to have)

Used to express possession or certain states.

- Wir **haben** ein Haus. (We have a house.)  
- Du **hast** Hunger. (You are hungry.)

### 3. Gehen (to go)

Commonly used to talk about movement or travel.

- Ich **gehe** zur Schule. (I go to school.)  
- Sie **geht** nach Hause. (She goes home.)

### 4. Machen (to do/make)

A versatile verb for actions and activities.

- Was **machst** du? (What are you doing?)  
- Er **macht** seine Hausaufgaben. (He is doing his homework.)

### 5. Sagen (to say)

Useful for reporting speech or opinions.

- Ich **sage** die Wahrheit. (I tell the truth.)  
- Sie **sagt**, dass sie kommt. (She says she is coming.)

### 6. Können (can/to be able to)

Expresses ability or permission.

- Ich **kann** Deutsch sprechen. (I can speak German.)  
- Kannst du mir helfen? (Can you help me?)

---

### Additional Useful Verbs

- **Wollen** (to want)  
- **Sehen** (to see)  
- **Kommen** (to come)  
- **Nehmen** (to take)

---

### Practical Tips for Learning German Verbs

- **Practice conjugation:** Start with the present tense forms of these verbs.  
- **Use flashcards:** Write the infinitive on one side and example sentences on the other.  
- **Make sentences daily:** Try to create simple sentences using each verb.  
- **Listen and repeat:** Hearing these verbs in context helps you remember them.
