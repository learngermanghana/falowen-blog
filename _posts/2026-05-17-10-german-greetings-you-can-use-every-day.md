## 10 German Greetings You Can Use Every Day

Learning common German greetings is the first step to starting conversations and making a great impression. Here are 10 essential German greetings that you can use daily, whether you're meeting friends, colleagues, or strangers.

### Basic Greetings

- **Hallo**  
  The most common and casual way to say "Hello." Use it any time of the day.

- **Guten Morgen**  
  Means "Good morning." Use it in the morning until around midday.

- **Guten Tag**  
  Translates to "Good day." A polite greeting for daytime, typically from late morning to early evening.

- **Guten Abend**  
  Means "Good evening." Use it after about 5 or 6 PM.

### Friendly and Informal Greetings

- **Hi**  
  Just like in English, "Hi" is informal and widely used among friends and younger people.

- **Servus**  
  A regional greeting common in southern Germany and Austria. It means both "hello" and "goodbye."

### Polite and Formal Greetings

- **Grüß Gott**  
  A formal greeting used mainly in southern Germany and Austria, meaning "God greet you."

- **Moin**  
  Common in northern Germany, it works for both "hello" and "goodbye," used all day.

### Other Useful Phrases

- **Wie geht’s?**  
  Means "How are you?" Often used right after a greeting.

- **Schön, dich/Sie zu sehen**  
  "Nice to see you." Use "dich" for informal and "Sie" for formal situations.

---

## Tips for Using German Greetings

- Pay attention to the time of day to choose the right greeting.
- Use formal greetings like "Guten Tag" or "Grüß Gott" in professional or respectful settings.
- Don’t hesitate to add a friendly follow-up like "Wie geht’s?" to keep the conversation going.
