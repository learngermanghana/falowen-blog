## German Numbers 1 to 20 Made Easy

Learning numbers is one of the first steps in mastering a new language. Today, we'll focus on **German numbers 1 to 20** — a foundation you’ll use every day!

### Why Learn German Numbers 1 to 20?

Numbers help you:
- Tell the time
- Count objects
- Give your phone number or address
- Understand prices and dates

### The Numbers 1 to 20 in German

| Number | German    | Pronunciation tip             |
|--------|-----------|------------------------------|
| 1      | eins      | "ines"                      |
| 2      | zwei      | "tsvai"                     |
| 3      | drei      | "dry"                       |
| 4      | vier      | "feer"                      |
| 5      | fünf      | "fuenf" (with a slight “ü” sound) |
| 6      | sechs     | "zeks"                      |
| 7      | sieben    | "zee-ben"                   |
| 8      | acht      | "acht" (like "aht" + a slight “ch” sound) |
| 9      | neun      | "noyn"                      |
| 10     | zehn      | "tsayn"                     |
| 11     | elf       | "elf"                       |
| 12     | zwölf     | "tsvölf"                    |
| 13     | dreizehn  | "dry-tsayn"                 |
| 14     | vierzehn  | "feer-tsayn"                |
| 15     | fünfzehn  | "fuenf-tsayn"               |
| 16     | sechzehn  | "zeks-tsayn"                |
| 17     | siebzehn  | "zeep-tsayn"                |
| 18     | achtzehn  | "acht-tsayn"                |
| 19     | neunzehn  | "noyn-tsayn"                |
| 20     | zwanzig   | "tsvan-tsig"                |

### Practical Examples Using Numbers 1 to 20

- Ich habe **drei** Äpfel.  
  *(I have three apples.)*

- Er wohnt in der Nummer **acht**.  
  *(He lives at number eight.)*

- Wir treffen uns um **zwölf** Uhr.  
  *(We meet at twelve o’clock.)*

- Sie kauft **fünfzehn** Bücher.  
  *(She buys fifteen books.)*

- Ich brauche **sieben** Stühle für die Gäste.  
  *(I need seven chairs for the guests.)*

- Das Hotelzimmer kostet **neunzehn** Euro pro Nacht.  
  *(The hotel room costs nineteen euros per night.)*

### Tips to Remember German Numbers 1 to 20

- Notice the pattern from 13 to 19: number + "zehn" (ten)  
- Practice pronunciation slowly and repeat regularly  
- Use flashcards with the number on one side and the German word on the other  
- Try counting objects around you in German
