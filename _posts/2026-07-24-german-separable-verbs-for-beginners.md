## German Separable Verbs: What Are Trennbare Verben?

If you are learning German at **A1 level**, you will quickly meet verbs such as **aufstehen**, **einkaufen**, **anrufen** and **fernsehen**. These are called **separable verbs** or **trennbare Verben** because part of the verb separates in many German sentences.

The basic idea is simple:

> **aufstehen** → Ich **stehe** um 7 Uhr **auf**.  
> I get up at 7 o'clock.

The main verb is conjugated and stays near the beginning of the sentence. The separable prefix moves to the end.

This guide explains the rule step by step and gives you practical examples you can use in everyday German.

## 1. How German Separable Verbs Work

Many separable verbs consist of two parts:

**prefix + base verb**

For example:

- **auf + stehen = aufstehen** — to get up
- **ein + kaufen = einkaufen** — to shop
- **an + rufen = anrufen** — to call
- **mit + kommen = mitkommen** — to come along
- **ab + fahren = abfahren** — to depart

In a normal present-tense main clause, conjugate the base verb and put the prefix at the end.

> Ich **rufe** meine Mutter **an**.  
> I call my mother.

> Wir **kaufen** am Samstag **ein**.  
> We shop on Saturday.

> Der Zug **fährt** um 10 Uhr **ab**.  
> The train departs at 10 o'clock.

A useful A1 formula is:

**Subject + conjugated verb + other information + prefix.**

## 2. Common Separable Prefixes in German

Some prefixes frequently appear in separable verbs. Common ones include **ab-**, **an-**, **auf-**, **aus-**, **ein-**, **mit-**, **vor-**, **weg-**, **zu-** and **zurück-**.

| Prefix | Example verb | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ab- | abfahren | to depart |
| an- | anrufen | to call |
| auf- | aufstehen | to get up |
| aus- | ausgehen | to go out |
| ein- | einkaufen | to shop |
| mit- | mitkommen | to come along |
| vor- | vorstellen | to introduce / present |
| weg- | weggehen | to go away |
| zu- | zumachen | to close |
| zurück- | zurückkommen | to come back |

Do not try to memorize every separable verb at once. Learn each new verb together with one short sentence.

For example, instead of memorizing only **anrufen**, learn:

> Ich **rufe** dich morgen **an**.

That helps you remember both the meaning and the word order.

## 3. Conjugating Separable Verbs in the Present Tense

The base verb follows the normal conjugation rules.

Take **aufstehen**:

| Person | Sentence |
|---|---|
| ich | Ich **stehe** um 7 Uhr **auf**. |
| du | Du **stehst** früh **auf**. |
| er/sie/es | Er **steht** um 6 Uhr **auf**. |
| wir | Wir **stehen** zusammen **auf**. |
| ihr | Ihr **steht** spät **auf**. |
| sie/Sie | Sie **stehen** jeden Tag früh **auf**. |

Notice that **auf** does not change. Only **stehen** is conjugated.

## 4. Separable Verbs in Questions

The prefix still moves to the end in a normal question.

### W-question

> Wann **stehst** du **auf**?  
> When do you get up?

> Wo **kaufst** du **ein**?  
> Where do you shop?

### Yes-or-no question

> **Rufst** du Anna **an**?  
> Are you calling Anna?

> **Kommst** du **mit**?  
> Are you coming along?

The conjugated verb moves according to the normal question rule, while the prefix remains at the end.

## 5. What Happens with Modal Verbs?

There is an important exception beginners should know.

When a modal verb such as **können**, **müssen**, **wollen** or **möchten** is used, the separable verb normally stays together in the infinitive at the end.

Compare:

> Ich **kaufe** heute **ein**.  
> I am shopping today.

But:

> Ich **möchte** heute **einkaufen**.  
> I would like to shop today.

Another example:

> Er **ruft** seine Freundin **an**.

With a modal verb:

> Er **muss** seine Freundin **anrufen**.

So do not separate the infinitive after a modal verb.

## 6. Separable Verbs in the Perfect Tense

At A1 and A2, you will also begin talking about past events with the **Perfekt**.

For many separable verbs, **ge-** appears between the prefix and the base verb:

- einkaufen → **eingekauft**
- aufmachen → **aufgemacht**
- anrufen → **angerufen**
- aufstehen → **aufgestanden**

Examples:

> Ich habe im Supermarkt **eingekauft**.

> Sie hat mich gestern **angerufen**.

> Wir sind früh **aufgestanden**.

Remember that the auxiliary verb can be **haben** or **sein**, depending on the verb.

Try creating one sentence about your own life with each verb. Personal examples are usually easier to remember than isolated vocabulary lists.

## 8. Common Mistakes with Trennbare Verben

### Mistake 1: Keeping the verb together in a main clause

Incorrect:

> Ich aufstehe um 7 Uhr.

Correct:

> Ich **stehe** um 7 Uhr **auf**.

### Mistake 2: Forgetting the prefix

Incorrect:

> Ich rufe meine Mutter.

If you mean “I call my mother,” use:

> Ich **rufe** meine Mutter **an**.

### Mistake 3: Separating the infinitive after a modal verb

Incorrect:

> Ich möchte heute kaufe ein.

Correct:

> Ich möchte heute **einkaufen**.

## 9. Quick Practice: Separable Verbs A1

Complete each sentence with the correct form.

1. Ich ______ um 6:30 Uhr ______. (**aufstehen**)
2. Wann ______ der Zug ______? (**abfahren**)
3. Maria ______ ihre Freundin ______. (**anrufen**)
4. Wir möchten heute ______. (**einkaufen**)
5. ______ du morgen ______? (**mitkommen**)

### Answers

1. Ich **stehe** um 6:30 Uhr **auf**.
2. Wann **fährt** der Zug **ab**?
3. Maria **ruft** ihre Freundin **an**.
4. Wir möchten heute **einkaufen**.
5. **Kommst** du morgen **mit**?

## 10. How to Learn Separable Verbs Faster

Learn the whole verb, its meaning and one example sentence together. Then say the example aloud and change the subject or time information.

For example:

> Ich stehe um 7 Uhr auf.  
> Meine Schwester steht um 6 Uhr auf.  
> Am Sonntag stehen wir spät auf.

This turns grammar into speaking practice instead of pure memorization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a separable verb in German?

A separable verb is a verb whose prefix separates from the base verb in certain sentence structures. For example, **aufstehen** becomes **Ich stehe auf** in a present-tense main clause.

### How do I know if a German verb is separable?

Many verbs beginning with prefixes such as **an-**, **auf-**, **aus-**, **ein-**, **mit-** and **zurück-** are separable. It is still best to learn each verb as a complete vocabulary item because not every prefix follows the same pattern.

### Do separable verbs always split?

No. With a modal verb, the separable verb usually remains together as an infinitive: **Ich muss einkaufen.** It also behaves differently in subordinate clauses and in participle forms.

### Is aufstehen a separable verb?

Yes. **Aufstehen** is one of the most common separable verbs: **Ich stehe jeden Morgen um 7 Uhr auf.**

### Are separable verbs important for German A1?

Yes. They appear frequently in A1 topics such as daily routines, shopping, transport, appointments and free time. Learning them early makes everyday German sentences much easier to understand and produce.
