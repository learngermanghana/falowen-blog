## German Numbers 1 to 20 Made Easy

Learning **German numbers 1 to 20** is a great first step in building your basic vocabulary. Numbers are everywhere—in phone numbers, prices, dates, and more. Let’s break them down together with clear examples.

### German Numbers 1 to 10

| Number | German   | Pronunciation Hint      |
|--------|----------|------------------------|
| 1      | eins     | [eyns]                 |
| 2      | zwei     | [tsvai]                |
| 3      | drei     | [dry]                  |
| 4      | vier     | [feer]                 |
| 5      | fünf     | [fünf] (with umlaut)   |
| 6      | sechs    | [zeks]                 |
| 7      | sieben   | [ZEE-ben]              |
| 8      | acht     | [akt]                  |
| 9      | neun     | [noyn]                 |
| 10     | zehn     | [tsayn]                |

### German Numbers 11 to 20

| Number | German      | Pronunciation Hint     |
|--------|-------------|-----------------------|
| 11     | elf         | [elf]                 |
| 12     | zwölf       | [tsvölf]              |
| 13     | dreizehn    | [dry-tsayn]           |
| 14     | vierzehn    | [feer-tsayn]          |
| 15     | fünfzehn    | [fünf-tsayn]          |
| 16     | sechzehn    | [zeks-tsayn]          |
| 17     | siebzehn    | [zeep-tsayn]          |
| 18     | achtzehn    | [akt-tsayn]           |
| 19     | neunzehn    | [noyn-tsayn]          |
| 20     | zwanzig     | [TSVAN-tsig]          |

### Practical Examples Using German Numbers 1 to 20

- Ich habe **drei** Äpfel. (I have three apples.)
- Wir treffen uns um **acht** Uhr. (We meet at eight o’clock.)
- Mein Bruder ist **zwölf** Jahre alt. (My brother is twelve years old.)
- Sie kauft **fünfzehn** Bücher. (She buys fifteen books.)
- Es sind **neunzehn** Grad heute. (It’s nineteen degrees today.)
- Ich möchte **sieben** Kekse, bitte. (I would like seven cookies, please.)

### Tips for Remembering German Numbers 1 to 20

- Notice that numbers 13 to 19 combine the base number + “zehn” (ten), similar to English “thirteen,” “fourteen,” etc.
- Pronunciations can be tricky—practice listening and repeating aloud.
- Use flashcards to match numbers with their German words.
- Try counting everyday objects in German to build confidence.
