## How to Ask Simple Questions in German

Asking questions is one of the first steps to having real conversations in German. Today, we focus on some of the most common German question words: **wo**, **was**, **wer**, and **wann**. These words help you gather important information about places, things, people, and time.

### Key German Question Words

- **Wo** – Where  
  Use this to ask about locations.  
  Example:  
  - Wo ist die Toilette? (Where is the bathroom?)  
  - Wo wohnst du? (Where do you live?)

- **Was** – What  
  Use this to ask about things or actions.  
  Example:  
  - Was machst du? (What are you doing?)  
  - Was ist das? (What is that?)

- **Wer** – Who  
  Use this to ask about people.  
  Example:  
  - Wer ist das? (Who is that?)  
  - Wer kommt mit? (Who is coming along?)

- **Wann** – When  
  Use this to ask about time.  
  Example:  
  - Wann beginnt der Film? (When does the movie start?)  
  - Wann hast du Geburtstag? (When is your birthday?)

### Tips for Asking Questions in German

- The question word usually comes at the very beginning of the sentence.  
- The verb follows immediately after the question word.  
- Keep your questions short and simple at first to build confidence.

### More Examples to Practice

- Wo arbeitet dein Freund? (Where does your friend work?)  
- Was möchtest du essen? (What do you want to eat?)  
- Wer ist dein Lehrer? (Who is your teacher?)  
- Wann fährt der Zug ab? (When does the train leave?)  
- Wo kann ich ein Taxi finden? (Where can I find a taxi?)  
- Was kostet das? (What does that cost?)
